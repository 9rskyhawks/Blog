Don't Develop Just for Yourself

A Developer's Checklist for Accessibility

Why Accessibility Matters

Accessibility ensures that everyone, including people with disabilities, can interact with and benefit from your application. Designing with accessibility in mind fosters inclusivity and improves the overall user experience.

Statistics show that over 1 billion people worldwide live with some form of disability. As developers, it’s our responsibility to make sure our work is usable by everyone.

Common Accessibility Issues

  • Text that lacks sufficient color contrast.
  • Images without meaningful alternative text (alt attributes).
  • Non-descriptive link text (e.g., "Click here").
  • Missing or improper use of semantic HTML elements.
  • Websites that are not keyboard navigable.

A Developer's Accessibility Checklist

  1. Use Semantic HTML: Ensure proper use of <header>, <main>, <footer>, <nav>, and <button> tags for better screen reader support.
  2. Provide Alternative Text: Add descriptive alt attributes for all images.
  3. Ensure Keyboard Navigation: Test your site to confirm users can navigate using only the keyboard.
  4. Check Color Contrast: Ensure text has sufficient contrast with the background using tools like WebAIM Contrast Checker.
  5. Use ARIA Roles Wisely: Apply ARIA roles when necessary to enhance accessibility, but don't overuse them.
  6. Design Responsive Interfaces: Test your application on different screen sizes and orientations.
  7. Include Captions and Transcripts: Provide captions for videos and transcripts for audio content.
  8. Test with Screen Readers: Use tools like NVDA or VoiceOver to ensure your app is screen reader-friendly.

Helpful Tools for Accessibility Testing

  • WAVE - Accessibility Evaluation Tool
  • axe DevTools - Chrome Extension for Accessibility Testing
  • Google Lighthouse - Performance and Accessibility Testing
  • W3C Evaluation Tools

Final Thoughts

Accessibility isn’t just about meeting legal requirements or guidelines; it’s about creating an inclusive experience for everyone. By following this checklist and incorporating accessibility into your workflow, you can ensure your web applications are welcoming to all users.

Remember, don't develop just for yourself—think about the diverse needs of your users!
